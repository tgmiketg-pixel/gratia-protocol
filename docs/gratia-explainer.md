# Gratia — Simple Explainer

## How It Works

You download an app on your phone. You just use your phone normally all day — texting, browsing, whatever. The app quietly notices that a real human is using a real phone (it checks things like "is the phone moving around?" and "is it connecting to Wi-Fi?"). It never looks at what you're doing — just that a real person is there. That's called **Proof of Life**.

Then at night, when you plug your phone in to charge, your phone does a little bit of work for the network — kind of like how your body heals while you sleep. It only works when your battery is above 80%, so it never hurts your phone. For doing that work, you earn coins called **GRAT**.

## Where Is It Stored?

There's no big computer in a building somewhere. Instead, every single phone keeps a small copy of the important information. When you send someone coins, all the phones agree "yep, that happened" and write it down together. It's like if every kid in school had the same notebook and they all wrote down the same thing at the same time — nobody can cheat because everyone would notice.

The data lives on millions of phones all over the world. No single person controls it.

## Why Is It Different?

- **You don't need an expensive computer.** Bitcoin needs giant warehouses full of machines. Gratia just needs the phone already in your pocket — even a cheap one.
- **It doesn't waste electricity.** Bitcoin uses as much power as some countries. Gratia only works when your phone is already charging.
- **One person = one vote.** Rich people can't buy more power. Every phone gets the same say. Every phone earns the same rate.
- **It knows you're real.** Other blockchains can be tricked by robots and fake accounts. Gratia checks that a real human is holding a real phone, so no one can cheat with a room full of fake phones.
- **It's private.** Your phone proves you're real without telling anyone anything about you. It's like raising your hand in class without anyone seeing your face.

## The One-Liner

**Bitcoin is a blockchain for warehouses. Gratia is a blockchain for people.**

## Security: Three Walls

To attack Gratia, you'd have to beat all three of these at the same time:

1. **Proof of Life** — You need thousands of real phones carried by real humans living real lives
2. **Staking** — You need to lock up tokens on every phone, subject to per-node caps
3. **Energy Expenditure** — Every phone must do real computation on real ARM hardware

Beating one wall is hard. Beating two is impractical. Beating all three at once costs more than you could ever gain from the attack.

## How You Earn

- Install the app
- Plug in your phone — mining starts immediately, no waiting period
- Battery must be above 80%
- Earn GRAT every minute your phone is mining
- Flat rate — no bonuses for expensive phones, no penalties for cheap ones
- Everyone earns the same
